Debt payoff can feel slow because the work is repetitive. You make payments, wait, check balances, and repeat.

Tracking helps because it turns that invisible effort into something you can see.

## Track balances, not just payments

Payments matter, but balances show the direction of travel. Once a month, write down:

1. Current balance
2. Interest rate
3. Minimum payment
4. Extra payment, if any

The goal is not to stare at the numbers every day. The goal is to give yourself a clear check-in point.

## Make progress visible

A progress bar, payoff chart, or simple line in a notebook can help you notice the movement that your nervous system might miss.

> Small visible progress can make a long payoff plan feel more possible.
