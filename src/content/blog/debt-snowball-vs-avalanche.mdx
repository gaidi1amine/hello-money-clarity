The debt snowball and debt avalanche are both useful. The best choice is usually the one that helps you keep going when the first burst of motivation wears off.

## Debt snowball

With the snowball method, you list debts from smallest balance to largest balance. You pay minimums on everything, then put extra money toward the smallest balance first.

This can feel encouraging because you get an early win. For many people, that momentum matters.

## Debt avalanche

With the avalanche method, you list debts from highest interest rate to lowest interest rate. You pay minimums on everything, then put extra money toward the highest-rate debt first.

This usually saves the most money mathematically, especially when one card has a much higher rate than the rest.

## A simple way to decide

Ask yourself what you need most right now:

- If you need motivation and visible progress, start with snowball.
- If interest charges are the pressure point, start with avalanche.
- If one debt is causing the most stress, consider making that your first target.

There is no shame in choosing the method you can repeat. Consistency does more for debt payoff than a perfect spreadsheet you avoid opening.
